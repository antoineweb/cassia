About Us

The first cinnamon co-op at the source

Cassia Co-op is the first and only cinnamon processing and exporting company established directly in Kerinci, Sumatra — the beating heart of the world's largest cinnamon plantation.

50% of the world's cinnamon originates from Indonesia. Most of it grows in one unique area on the island of Sumatra, called Kerinci. We created a fair and efficient supply chain while generating a sustainable, positive impact on the local community.

No middlemen. No guesswork. Just a direct line from over 1,000 partner farmers to your production facility — with full traceability at every step.

Our Story

Born in Kerinci,
built for the world

"We didn't choose the location. The cinnamon chose it, thousands of years ago. We just set up where it makes sense."

The Kerinci Valley in Jambi Province, Sumatra, is home to the world's most extensive cassia cinnamon plantations. Blessed with volcanic soil, a tropical climate, and generations of farming expertise, this region produces cinnamon unlike anywhere else on earth.

Cassia Co-op was founded on a simple but radical idea: what if the processor, the quality controller, and the exporter were all based right here, at the source? What if the farmers and the company shared the same land, the same community, and the same future?

Today, we partner with over 1,000 local farming families, manage our own FSSC 22000 certified processing facility, and export directly to clients in Europe, the United States, and across Asia Pacific — all without a single unnecessary intermediary.
